The Core Workflow: How to Edit a Video Step by Step

Step 1: Organise Before You Touch the Timeline

Create folders for raw footage, audio, music, B-roll, graphics and exports. Rename clips clearly.

Step 2: Build a Rough Cut

Structure your video into beginning (hook), middle (value/story) and end (conclusion or CTA).

Step 3: Tighten the Edit

Remove long pauses, repeated phrases and filler words. If it feels slow, it probably is.

Step 4: Edit Audio Before Colour

Fix dialogue levels, reduce noise and balance music. Keep dialogue peaking around -6 dB.

Step 5: Colour Correction, Then Colour Grading

Correct exposure and white balance first. Then apply creative grading.

Step 6: Add Graphics and Captions

Captions increase retention. Keep typography clean and readable.

Step 7: Export with Proper Settings

Use H.264 format, correct resolution and balanced bitrate for best results.

FAQs

What are the most important video editing tips?

Focus on clean cuts, strong audio, consistent colour correction, intentional pacing and correct export settings.

How do beginners edit videos?

Start with a rough cut, remove mistakes, fix audio, correct colour, then export properly.

What makes a video look professional?

Good audio, stable pacing, natural colour balance and clean storytelling.

What frame rate looks cinematic?

24fps is commonly used for cinematic style.

Is video editing hard to learn?

The basics are easy. Mastery takes practice.
